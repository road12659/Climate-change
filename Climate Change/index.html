What is Climate Change?

Climate change refers to long-term shifts in temperatures and weather patterns across the Earth. While natural factors like changes in the sun's activity or volcanic eruptions can
cause some shifts, human activities have been the primary
driver of climate change since the 1800s.

Global Temperature Rise

The planet's average surface temperature has risen by approximately 1.1°C (about 2°F) since the late 19th century. Most of this warming has occurred in the past 40 years, with the last decade being the warmest on record. In fact, 2024 was confirmed as the warmest year on record, exceeding the pre-industrial average by about 1.55°C.

High Levels of Greenhouse Gases

While Earth's climate has changed throughout its history, the current warming trend is happening at a rate not seen in the past 10,000 years. Scientific evidence from natural sources like ice cores, tree rings, and ocean sediments, combined with modern equipment like satellites, confirms this accelerated warming.

The Ocean As A Giant Heat Sink

The ocean plays a critical role in regulating Earth's temperature. It has absorbed over 90% of the excess heat caused by climate change, with the top 100 meters of the ocean warming significantly since 1969. This absorption of heat is a major reason why global land temperatures haven't risen even more dramatically

Why Help Tackle Climate Change?

The Future Generation Shall Face The Consequences

Today's youth and future generations will disproportionately bear the brunt of climate change impacts.
This includes more frequent and intense heatwaves, floods, droughts, food and water scarcity, sea-level
rise, and the spread of diseases. Understanding these threats is vital for their own well-being and future

Youth bring unique perspectives, creativity, passion, and energy to the climate conversation.
They are less bound by traditional thinking and are adept at using digital technologies to
raise awareness, organize movements, and propose innovative solutions. Their voices are
crucial in pushing for ambitious policies and practices.
