Causes of Climate Change

Burning Fossil Fuels

This is the largest contributor. When we burn coal, oil,
and natural gas for electricity, heating, manufacturing,
and transportation (cars, trucks, planes, ships), we
release vast amounts of carbon dioxide (CO2​).

Deforestation

Forests act as "carbon sinks," absorbing CO2​ from
the atmosphere. When trees are cut down for
agriculture, logging, or development, the stored
carbon is released, and the Earth's capacity to
absorb future emissions is reduced.

Industrial Process

Manufacturing cement, iron, steel, plastics, and
other goods requires significant energy, often
from burning fossil fuels. Some industrial
processes also release greenhouse gases directly.

Effects of Climate Change

Rising Global Temperatures

The Earth's average surface temperature
has risen significantly, leading to more
frequent and intense heatwaves.

Droughts

Prolonged periods of dryness leading to
water scarcity, impacting agriculture,
livelihoods, and increasing the risk of
wildfires. Parts of Ghana are vulnerable
to drought, affecting food security.

Heavy Rainfall and Flooding

Leading to displacement, property
damage, and agricultural disruption.
Ghana, for example, experiences
significant flooding, particularly in
urban areas and coastal regions.

Solutions To Climate Change

Transition to Renewable Energy

Shifting away from fossil fuels to
clean energy sources like solar,
wind, hydropower, and geothermal
energy for electricity generation,
transportation, and industrial processes.

Waste Reduction and Management

Reducing energy consumption in homes,
buildings, and transportation through
better insulation, energy-efficient appliances,
public transport, cycling, and walking.

Sustainable Land Use and Forestry

Reducing consumption, reusing, recycling,
and composting to minimize waste going
to landfills and producing methane.
